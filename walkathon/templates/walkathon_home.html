DID YOU KNOW?...

Youth attempted suicide rates in New Jersey rose to 5.9%
as of 2019, and is highest among Black youth at 11.3%

Black adults below the poverty line are 2x more likely
to report serious psychological distress than others

Nearly 65% of Black youth report traumatic racial
experiences, contributing to higher rates of
PTSD, depression, and anxiety

Unfortunately, 2 out of 3 Black adults with mental illness
do not receive treatment

In an effort to promote mental and physical wellness while highlighting national Minority Mental Health Month in July, ETV is hosting its inaugural Power Walk on July 15, 2023 at South Mountain Reservation in West Orange, New Jersey.

Data shows that regular physical exercise and time outdoors greatly improve mental health. This event will take place from 8AM until 12PM with a vigorous 1.8 mile walk around the lake (or virtually), with an expert mental healthcare provider, and other activities! Sponsor the event or an individual Power Walker today!

Support in promoting the Power Walk for Mental Health as a sponsor, enlisting employee participation as well as funding to support the Village Assist Program.

ETV is developing this platform to provide the Black community with access to free and affordable mental health services, and connection to Black psychiatrists, psychologists, therapists, social workers and other wellness professionals. ETV is committed to helping eradicate the stigma and financial barriers to seeking mental health services, which are challenges impacting large swaths of the Black community. Culturally competent professionals are critical to ensuring successful outcomes for Black adults and youth seeking to improve their emotional health and well-being.
