State Of The Village : Did You Know...?

Black people comprise ~14% of the US population, yet we only hold 3% (3 of 100) of US Senate seats

Black women are among the most engaged and influential voting blocs in the country. Their expanding influence and political power will likely shape the trajectory of America for generations

While Black Americans make up ~14% of the US population, they account for ~33% of the US incarcerated population and ~23% of people killed by police

Compared to the pre-Civil Rights era, many more Black people now have significant financial resources and hold positions of power that far exceed what their ancestors may have envisioned only a few decades after their hard-fought victories. Yet, we have not fully leveraged our abundant political and economic clout and power, and far too many of us are still suffering from the same challenges that persisted prior to those advances. Despite encouraging progress, there have also been many disappointing setbacks.
