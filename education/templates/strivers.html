Education Initiatives
A college degree, whether an Associate or Bachelor’s degree, is more and more vital. The number of “good-paying jobs” that did not require some level of college education or technical training have continued to rapidly decrease.
Village@Work™: This workforce development program is focused on preparing business owners to create jobs and oppportunities for the communities they serve. It includes ETV support for establishing internships,apprenticeships, part-time and full-time jobs within Black-owned businesses that need additional personnel to enhance their success. It creates a win-win for our business owners, job/career seekers, and the economy.
Village Strivers™: This ETV program provides academic enrichment, homework help, coaching and tutoring, test prep, resources, and financial support for college-bound youth as well as job training for adults and youth (virtually or in-person).
The program is implemented in partnership with Village volunteers and paid teachers of color who serve as role model, mentors, and academic coaches. For eligible registrants, ETV subsidizes the cost of individual and group sessions with enrichment providers and independent tutors, emphasizing math, science, writing, and public speaking skills.

Support The Linda D. Murphy Village Strivers Scholarship Fund

Morristown, NJ Educator, Non-profit Executive, and Community Leader, the late Linda D. Murphy, was the inspiration for ETV’s Village Strivers program. She brought her passion and commitment to youth to ETV, where she was instrumental to the vision and direction of our Village Strivers program. ETV is proud to have created The Linda D. Murphy Village Strivers Scholarship Fund, administered by the Community Foundation of New Jersey, to help seed the college aspirations of deserving high school students who show academic improvement by awarding them a scholarship in honor of Linda Murphy’s exemplary life.
