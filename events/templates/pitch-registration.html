What is Village Ventures™?

The goal of ETV's Inaugural Village Ventures Pitch Competition is helping prepare Black Business Owners to grow their companies more successfully by:

  • Creating visibility for and awareness around their products and services
  • Encouraging them to refine their marketing plans and strategies
  • Providing them with free access to 1-on-1 strategic business counseling funded by ETV, and
  • Awarding $10,000 in monetary prizes to the finalists.
Top 5 finalists will present their pitch live at the Empowerment Expo on February 11. All 5 Finalists will receive a cash prize.

Winners will be announced at the Expo!

Competition Overview

The Village Ventures Pitch Competition has two phases. In the first phase all participants will be evaluated to select five finalists to pitch live for cash prizes in the second phase.

What do you need to submit?

  • Executive Summary (maximum 3 pages/slides)
    • Background information on you/your business
    • Marketing Plan
    • Specific plans for how you intend to use the cash award to grow, sustain, or enhance your business if awarded

Executive Summary - Business/Marketing Plan outline will be provided upon receipt of competition fees.

  • 60-90 second video introducing yourself and your business, including how you intend to use the cash award for your business
  • Your photo (and product or location photos if applicable)
  • Completion of a VEN Business Counseling Session

Complete application due by noon on February 3rd

Registration Requirements

Applicants must be an active Black-owned business with at least 51% equity or ownership held by the Black owner(s)

Enroll in ETV’s Village Empowerment Network™ (VEN)

Be an active business with history of sales (at least $2,500) to demonstrate viability

Submit competition application materials using the form below, or email your submission to admin@empowerthevillage.org

Pay the $50 application fee by Jan 28th

Phase 1: Preliminary Round

Judges will evaluate all participant submissions and select 5 finalists to compete in Phase 2. Finalists will be announced on February 6th.

Phase 2: Final Round

Candidates with the top 5 scores during the Preliminary Round of judging will be asked to present their business pitch in-person at ETV's Empowerment Expo on Saturday, Feb 11 at Bloomfield College:

  • Up to 2-minute pitch followed by Q&A
  • No slides, but props are allowed
  • Cash prizes for all 5 finalists

Top 3 winners will be announced at the Expo!

The Competition is open to only legal residents of the fifty (50) United States and the District of Columbia who at the time of entry are independent owners/operators of an eligible for-profit small business (revenue >$1M) that is domiciled in the fifty (50) United States or District of Columbia which has been in continuous operation selling a product or service, with revenue of at least $2,500. In order to be considered an eligible “small business” for purposes of this Competition, the business must meet all of the following criteria:

  1. Entrant/owner must be at least eighteen (18) years of age at the time of Entry Submission;
